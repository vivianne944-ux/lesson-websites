Your answer key for all 32 cases

Each case contains the calculation used to determine the dose or quantity, followed by the prescription body in the format taught in the solid and liquid prescription-writing transes.

Apply this to every prescription: Add the date and the prescriber’s signature, printed name followed by M.D., clinic address and telephone number, PRC license number, and PTR number. Add the appropriate S2 number and No refills for regulated drugs. The case-specific answers below focus on the patient block, inscription, subscription, and signatura.
Source issues are not hidden. Cases 20, 23, and 24 contain incomplete or conflicting information. The relevant assumptions are stated directly in those answers so you can compare them with your instructor’s preferred interpretation.

Computation and rationale

  1. Daily dose = 10 kg × 20 mg/kg/day = 200 mg/day.
  2. Dose per intake = 200 mg/day ÷ 2 = 100 mg/dose.
  3. Total doses = 2/day × 7 days = 14 doses.

Although one dose is below 600 mg, the patient is only two years old. Following the pediatric paper examples in the trans, papers are used rather than capsules.
